# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Hiring managers, technical recruiters, and fellow senior engineers evaluating whether Lee can build and ship production-grade distributed systems. They arrive with skepticism and a short attention span — they scan for evidence, not prose.

## Product Purpose

Prove production competence through architecture, not claims. Every section, metric, and link must answer the unspoken question: "Did this person actually build something that works at scale, or are they just listing technologies?"

Success: the visitor opens a live service, reads a metric, or asks the chatbot a question and gets a real answer about a real system.

## Brand Personality

**Confident, precise, understated.**

- Confident: real metrics, live services, no hedging language
- Precise: architecture-first framing, exact numbers, correct terminology
- Understated: the work speaks; no superlatives, no passion paragraphs, no skill progress bars

Voice: senior engineer talking to another senior engineer. Assume the reader knows what a WebSocket is.

## Anti-references

- Generic dev portfolio templates: gradient hero, floating code snippets, terminal typing animations
- SaaS landing pages: pricing grids, trust logos, "seamless/scalable/enterprise-grade" word soup
- Junior portfolio clichés: "I love coding," skill progress bars, GitHub contribution graphs
- Agency portfolios: full-bleed video backgrounds, scrolljacking, page transitions as the product
- The AI developer aesthetic: green-on-black terminal, matrix rain, `>` prompts everywhere

## Design Principles

1. **Show the architecture, not the aesthetic.** The design's job is to present the systems clearly, not to be the attraction. Restraint wins.

2. **Every number needs a system behind it.** "10,000 concurrent connections" only works because the trade engine exists. No vanity metrics.

3. **One page, five decisions.** The visitor should leave knowing: what Lee builds, at what scale, with what tools, and whether to contact him. No scrolling through 8 sections to find the point.

4. **Live beats static.** Links go to running services. The chatbot answers from real code. Nothing on this page is mock data.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG 2.1 AA minimum. This is a text-and-layout site read by technical professionals — the bar is high-contrast, keyboard-navigable, screen-reader-clean. Reduced motion for vestibular disorders. No auto-playing anything.
